Computer graphics is a field that combines math, physics, programming, and art to create breathtaking scenery and immersive experiences. Whether you're a regular gamer with little to no programming knowledge, or a professional, or anything in between, whether you're just learning it or working on a real project, graphics programming is always rewarding because you get to visually see the changes your code makes. You get to generate photorealistic images literally from scratch, and this is several times more fun when you actually know what you're doing, when you stop needing to watch tutorials constantly and come up with new, more efficient, algorithms yourself.

What Is This?

This is a series of articles with the goal of making intuitive explanations on how things work under the hood. I am taking a "rediscovery" approach rather than a "feed in" approach. Instead of telling you what you should do to achieve X, we start from scratch and do things naively. We keep falling into problems and solving them together. This teaches you the reason why we do it this way and not that way, and why this method is bad and this one is good in a relatable way.

Who Is This For?

While it's certainly more biased towards beginners, I'm positive those with different levels of understanding will learn something new as well, or gain a deeper intuition about some concepts.

What You Need To Know Beforehand

If you know high school math, second-degree polynomials, basic calculus, and vector math, you're good. If you want to learn vector math and linear algebra in a visual way and intuitively, I highly, highly suggest you watch 3Blue1Brown's free video tutorial series on linear algebra . I dare say it's a must-watch for everyone.

A tiny amount of programming skills will also help you along the way. If you know absolutely nothing about programming, I suggest you learn Python as it's really straightforward.

If you're not familiar with some of these, I encourage you to still continue reading the articles, as I'll mention highly valuable sources for learning the necessary topics.
